AI-assisted report review for confident reporting

Accurate Digits was a software project exploring how AI-assisted analysis could support the review of structured, data-heavy reports such as financial, management, and operational reports.

Accurate Digits is no longer commercially available. No accounts, access, services, or support are provided. This page is maintained as an archival overview of the project and its approach.

Overview

Accurate Digits examined how automated analysis could help professionals identify areas that may warrant closer attention during review, particularly in environments where accuracy, consistency, and time pressure are critical. The intent was to support professional judgement, not replace it.

AI-assisted review approach

Accurate Digits explored how AI techniques could be applied to support the review of structured reports by identifying patterns and relationships that commonly require human verification.

In broad terms, the approach examined how AI could be used to:

  • Analyse structured, tabular report content
  • Identify relationships across rows, columns, and related values
  • Highlight patterns or inconsistencies that may warrant review
  • Surface areas of potential interest for further human assessment
  • Present findings in a clear, review-oriented format

The objective was to reduce repetitive manual checking while keeping professional judgement central to all review decisions.

Types of reports explored

During development, the approach was explored across a range of report types commonly used in professional and operational settings. The common characteristic was structured, tabular data where consistency and accuracy are essential.

  • Financial statements and supporting schedules
  • Management and board reports
  • KPI and performance summaries
  • Forecasts and planning models
  • Internal operational reports
  • Working papers and reconciliations

Context and purpose

Accurate Digits was developed as an exploration into how modern AI techniques could assist with review-heavy workflows. The project drew on experience in finance, audit, and data-intensive environments, where small discrepancies can have outsized downstream impact.

While the product itself is no longer active, the project remains a reference example of how AI can be applied thoughtfully to support accuracy, efficiency, and confidence in professional reporting.
